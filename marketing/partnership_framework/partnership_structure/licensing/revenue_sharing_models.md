# MultiOS Revenue Sharing and Licensing Models

## Overview

MultiOS offers flexible revenue sharing and licensing models designed to accommodate diverse institutional needs, budgets, and partnership objectives. Our goal is to create sustainable partnerships that benefit both educational institutions and the broader MultiOS ecosystem.

## 1. LICENSING MODELS

### 1.1 Educational Institutional License (EIL)
**Most Common Model for Universities and Colleges**

#### Structure:
- **Scope:** Institutional use across all departments and programs
- **Coverage:** Students, faculty, staff, and authorized researchers
- **Duration:** Annual license with multi-year options
- **Flexibility:** Overflow licenses available for enrollment fluctuations

#### Pricing Structure:
```
Bronze Tier: $5 per student per year
Silver Tier: $3.75 per student per year  
Gold Tier: $2.50 per student per year
Platinum Tier: $2 per student per year
```

#### Volume Discounts:
- **1,000-2,500 students:** 5% discount
- **2,501-5,000 students:** 10% discount
- **5,001-10,000 students:** 15% discount
- **10,001+ students:** 20% discount

#### Minimum Annual Commitment:
- Bronze: $2,500 minimum
- Silver: $7,500 minimum
- Gold: $25,000 minimum
- Platinum: $50,000 minimum

### 1.2 Course-Based Licensing (CBL)
**Ideal for Specific Program Offerings**

#### Structure:
- **Scope:** Licensed for specific courses or programs only
- **Coverage:** Enrolled students only for designated courses
- **Tracking:** Course enrollment-based usage monitoring
- **Scalability:** Easy to add or remove courses

#### Pricing Structure:
```
Per Student Per Course: $25-$75 (depending on tier)
Course Setup Fee: $500-$1,500 (one-time, tier-dependent)
```

#### Additional Benefits:
- No minimum student commitment
- Flexible course scheduling
- Easy program expansion
- Comprehensive course materials included

### 1.3 Department License (DL)
**Targeted Approach for Specific Departments**

#### Structure:
- **Scope:** Single academic department or school
- **Coverage:** All students, faculty, and staff within department
- **Exclusivity:** No cross-departmental usage without additional licensing
- **Management:** Department-level administration and control

#### Pricing Structure:
```
Flat Annual Fee Based on Department Size:
- Small (100-500 people): $15,000-$25,000
- Medium (501-1,500 people): $25,000-$50,000  
- Large (1,501+ people): $50,000-$100,000
```

### 1.4 Network License (NL)
**For Institution Networks and Consortia**

#### Structure:
- **Scope:** Multiple institutions under single agreement
- **Coverage:** All member institutions and their students
- **Management:** Centralized administration and billing
- **Benefits:** Shared resources and best practices

#### Pricing Structure:
```
Base Network Fee: $100,000 annually
Per Institution Fee: $10,000 annually per institution
Volume Discounts: 15-30% based on network size
```

## 2. REVENUE SHARING MODELS

### 2.1 Student Success Revenue Share
**Participate in Student Outcomes**

#### Model Description:
Institutions earn revenue based on student success metrics and career outcomes.

#### Calculation Formula:
```
Annual Revenue Share = (Base Fee × Success Multiplier × Enrollment Factor)
```

#### Success Metrics:
- **Course Completion Rate:** 85%+ = 1.2x multiplier
- **Student Satisfaction:** 4.0+ rating = 1.1x multiplier  
- **Industry Placement:** 80%+ placement within 6 months = 1.3x multiplier
- **Advanced Certification:** 60%+ pursuing advanced certification = 1.15x multiplier

#### Example Calculation:
- Base Fee: $50,000
- Completion Rate: 90% (1.2x)
- Satisfaction: 4.2/5.0 (1.1x)
- Placement: 85% (1.3x)
- Advanced Cert: 65% (1.15x)

**Revenue Share: $50,000 × 1.2 × 1.1 × 1.3 × 1.15 = $97,902**

### 2.2 Research Collaboration Revenue Share
**Share in Research Outcomes and Publications**

#### Model Description:
Institutions receive revenue sharing for joint research projects, publications, and innovations.

#### Revenue Sources:
- **Joint Publications:** $5,000-$25,000 per publication
- **Patent Applications:** $10,000-$100,000 per application (split 50/50)
- **Conference Presentations:** $2,000-$10,000 per presentation
- **Grant Applications:** 2% of total grant value for MultiOS-related research

#### Requirements:
- Active research collaboration agreement
- Proper attribution and citation
- Sharing of research methodologies and results
- Participation in MultiOS research consortium

### 2.3 Curriculum Development Revenue Share
**Earn from Developed Educational Content**

#### Model Description:
Institutions receive compensation for curriculum, lab exercises, and educational resources they develop.

#### Revenue Opportunities:
- **Curriculum Development:** $10,000-$50,000 per complete curriculum
- **Lab Exercise Creation:** $1,000-$5,000 per exercise set
- **Assessment Tools:** $5,000-$25,000 per assessment suite
- **Tutorial and Documentation:** $2,000-$10,000 per complete tutorial

#### Requirements:
- High-quality educational content
- Compatibility with MultiOS platform
- Peer review and approval process
- Licensing agreement for MultiOS use

### 2.4 Community Contribution Revenue Share
**Recognize and Reward Community Engagement**

#### Model Description:
Institutions earn revenue through active participation in the MultiOS educational community.

#### Contribution Types and Rewards:
- **Faculty Participation:** $1,000-$5,000 per year for active community engagement
- **Student Projects:** $500-$2,500 for exceptional student projects showcased
- **Best Practices:** $1,000-$3,000 for documented best practices
- **Mentorship:** $2,000-$10,000 for mentoring other institutions
