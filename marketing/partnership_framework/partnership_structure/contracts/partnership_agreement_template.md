# MultiOS Educational Partnership Agreement Framework

## PARTNERSHIP AGREEMENT TEMPLATE

This Educational Partnership Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into on [DATE] ("Effective Date") between [INSTITUTION NAME], a [STATE] [INSTITUTION TYPE] located at [ADDRESS] ("Institution"), and MultiOS Foundation, a [STATE] nonprofit corporation located at [MULTIOS ADDRESS] ("MultiOS").

## 1. PARTNERSHIP OVERVIEW

### 1.1 Partnership Purpose
This Agreement establishes a strategic partnership between Institution and MultiOS to promote computer science education through the use, implementation, and advancement of MultiOS educational platform and curriculum.

### 1.2 Partnership Tier
Institution is admitted as a [TIER LEVEL] Partner, with the following characteristics:
- **Student Capacity:** [NUMBER] students
- **License Type:** Educational Institutional License
- **Support Level:** [SUPPORT LEVEL] support package
- **Annual Commitment:** [NUMBER] courses/program offerings

### 1.3 Term
This Agreement shall commence on [START DATE] and continue for an initial term of [DURATION] years, unless terminated earlier as provided herein.

## 2. PARTNERSHIP BENEFITS AND OBLIGATIONS

### 2.1 MultiOS Obligations

#### 2.1.1 Software Licensing
- Provide educational licenses for MultiOS platform to Institution
- Grant access to MultiOS educational resources and documentation
- Provide software updates and maintenance as specified in tier benefits
- Ensure compliance with educational use restrictions

#### 2.1.2 Training and Support
- Provide faculty training as specified in partnership tier
- Offer technical support according to service level agreements
- Provide curriculum resources and educational materials
- Facilitate access to MultiOS educational community

#### 2.1.3 Research and Innovation Support
- Support research collaborations as outlined in partnership tier
- Provide access to beta features and development roadmap
- Facilitate student and faculty research opportunities
- Support conference presentations and publication efforts

### 2.2 Institution Obligations

#### 2.2.1 Educational Commitment
- Implement MultiOS curriculum in accordance with agreed timeline
- Provide faculty training and professional development
- Maintain minimum course offerings as specified in partnership tier
- Participate in MultiOS educational community and events

#### 2.2.2 Reporting and Assessment
- Provide quarterly reports on usage and student outcomes
- Participate in annual partnership assessment and evaluation
- Submit student and faculty feedback surveys
- Maintain accurate enrollment and usage records

#### 2.2.3 Financial Obligations
- Pay partnership fees according to payment schedule
- Provide required budget documentation and approvals
- Maintain current payment status throughout partnership
- Report on additional funding sources and grants obtained

## 3. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY TERMS

### 3.1 MultiOS Intellectual Property
- Institution acknowledges MultiOS ownership of platform and curriculum
- Educational use license granted for term of Agreement
- No rights to commercial use or redistribution
- Attribution requirements for educational materials

### 3.2 Institution Intellectual Property
- Institution retains ownership of institutional curriculum and materials
- Institution grants MultiOS limited rights to use anonymized feedback
- Right to publish success stories and case studies (with approval)
- Permission to use Institution as reference (with approval)

### 3.3 Joint Development
- Jointly developed materials will be co-owned
- Each party may use joint materials for educational purposes
- Commercial use requires mutual written consent
- Attribution requirements for joint publications

## 4. FINANCIAL TERMS

### 4.1 Partnership Fees
- **Annual Partnership Fee:** $[AMOUNT]
- **Payment Schedule:** [PAYMENT TERMS]
- **Additional Services:** As specified in Addendum A
- **Currency:** All fees in USD unless otherwise specified

### 4.2 Payment Terms
- Net 30 days from invoice date
- Late payment subject to 1.5% monthly interest
- Annual fee increases limited to 5% or CPI, whichever is less
- Price protection for multi-year agreements

### 4.3 Financial Reporting
- Institution agrees to provide financial status updates
- MultiOS provides annual financial statements for transparency
- Audit rights for compliance verification
- Special pricing for financial hardship cases

## 5. SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS

### 5.1 Technical Support Response Times
- **Bronze:** Email response within 24-48 hours
- **Silver:** Email response within 12-24 hours
- **Gold:** Phone and email response within 4-8 hours
- **Platinum:** Phone and email response within 1-2 hours

### 5.2 Software Availability
- 99.5% uptime guarantee for educational platform
- Scheduled maintenance windows with 48-hour notice
- Emergency maintenance procedures and communication
- Data backup and recovery guarantees

### 5.3 Training and Development
- Mandatory faculty training for all partnership tiers
- Annual certification renewal requirements
- Access to continuing education opportunities
- Faculty exchange and visiting scholar programs

## 6. DATA PRIVACY AND SECURITY

### 6.1 Student Data Protection
- FERPA compliance requirements and procedures
- Data minimization and purpose limitation principles
- Secure transmission and storage protocols
- Right to deletion and data portability

### 6.2 Institutional Data
- Protection of proprietary institutional information
- Access controls and authentication requirements
- Audit trails and monitoring procedures
- Incident response and breach notification

### 6.3 Third-Party Sharing
- No sharing of student or institutional data with third parties
- Aggregated and anonymized data may be used for research
- Clear opt-out procedures for data usage
- Regular privacy impact assessments

## 7. PERFORMANCE METRICS AND EVALUATION

### 7.1 Success Metrics
- Student enrollment and completion rates
- Faculty training and certification levels
- Technical support usage and satisfaction
- Research output and publications
- Community engagement and contributions

### 7.2 Reporting Requirements
- Quarterly partnership reports
- Annual comprehensive assessment
- Student outcome evaluations
- Faculty satisfaction surveys
- Financial status updates

### 7.3 Performance Review
- Annual partnership review meetings
- Performance improvement plans if needed
- Recognition for outstanding contributions
- Best practices sharing and documentation
- Continuous improvement initiatives

## 8. TERMINATION AND RENEWAL

### 8.1 Termination Rights
- Either party may terminate with 90 days written notice
- Termination for material breach with 30 days cure period
- Termination for financial non-payment after 60 days
- Termination for regulatory or legal compliance issues

### 8.2 Wind-Down Procedures
- 180-day wind-down period for program completion
- Continued access to resources during transition
- Data export and migration support
- Faculty training completion for current students

### 8.3 Renewal Process
- Renewal discussions begin 6 months before expiration
- Performance evaluation and tier reassessment
- Updated pricing and terms negotiation
- Multi-year renewal incentives

## 9. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

### 9.1 Good Faith Negotiations
- Direct negotiation between authorized representatives
- 30-day period for good faith resolution attempts
- Documentation of disputes and resolution attempts
- Preservation of business relationship during process

### 9.2 Mediation
- Binding mediation if direct negotiations fail
- Neutral mediator selected by mutual agreement
- Cost-sharing arrangement for mediation services
- Confidential mediation process

### 9.3 Arbitration
- Final and binding arbitration for unresolved disputes
- American Arbitration Association rules apply
- Location and procedures mutually agreed upon
- Enforceability under applicable arbitration laws

## 10. GENERAL PROVISIONS

### 10.1 Governing Law
- [STATE] law governs this Agreement
- Federal law for educational compliance matters
- International agreements for global partnerships
- Conflict of laws provisions

### 10.2 Force Majeure
- Excuse from performance for unforeseen circumstances
- Notification requirements for force majeure events
- Reasonable efforts to minimize impact
- Termination rights for extended force majeure

### 10.3 Assignment and Transfer
- No assignment without written consent
- Institution status change notification requirements
- MultiOS merger or acquisition provisions
- Student protection during institutional transitions

### 10.4 Amendment Procedures
- Written amendments signed by authorized representatives
- Academic calendar consideration for effective dates
- Faculty and student notification requirements
- Version control and change documentation
