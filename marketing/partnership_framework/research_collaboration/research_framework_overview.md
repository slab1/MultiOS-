# MultiOS Research Collaboration Framework

## Overview

The MultiOS Research Collaboration Framework establishes structured pathways for educational institutions to engage in meaningful research partnerships that advance operating systems education, drive technological innovation, and create measurable impact in academia and industry. Our framework is designed to accommodate institutions of all sizes and research capabilities while ensuring mutual benefit and sustainable collaboration.

### 1.2 Research Partnership Tiers

#### Level 1: Basic Research Partner
```
Eligibility Requirements:
- Active MultiOS educational partnership
- Commitment to contribute research findings
- Minimum one research project per year
- Participation in annual research survey

Benefits and Opportunities:
- Access to research version of MultiOS platform
- Technical support for research projects
- Annual research collaboration meeting
- Publication support and guidance
- Conference presentation opportunities
- Research funding information and guidance

Expected Contributions:
- Research project documentation and results
- Annual research report submission
- Participation in MultiOS research community
- Sharing of best practices and methodologies
- Student researcher participation in programs
```

#### Level 2: Advanced Research Partner
```
Eligibility Requirements:
- Active Gold or Platinum MultiOS educational partnership
- Demonstrated research track record
- Commitment to multiple research projects annually
- Faculty expertise in relevant research areas

Benefits and Opportunities:
- All Level 1 benefits plus:
- Priority access to new MultiOS features and capabilities
- Direct collaboration with MultiOS research team
- Joint grant application opportunities
- Custom research support and consultation
- Research equipment and resource sharing
- Publication in MultiOS research journal

Expected Contributions:
- Regular research publication and presentation
- Mentorship of student researchers
- Faculty exchange and visiting programs
- Best practices documentation and sharing
- Research methodology development and validation
- Technology transfer and commercialization support
```

#### Level 3: Strategic Research Partner
```
Eligibility Requirements:
- Elite research institution with outstanding track record
- Significant ongoing research collaboration with MultiOS
- Leadership role in MultiOS research community
- Commitment to long-term strategic partnership

Benefits and Opportunities:
- All Level 2 benefits plus:
- Co-development of MultiOS platform features
- Joint research center establishment
- Executive-level research collaboration
- Major grant application leadership
- Patent application and IP development
- International research partnership facilitation

Expected Contributions:
- Leadership in MultiOS research community

- International collaboration and partnership
```
