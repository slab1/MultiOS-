# MultiOS Educational Community Guidelines

## Code of Conduct

Our community is committed to providing a welcoming and inclusive environment for all learners and contributors.

### Our Values

- **Respect**: Treat all community members with respect and dignity
- **Inclusivity**: Welcome learners from all backgrounds and experience levels
- **Collaboration**: Work together to achieve common goals
- **Learning**: Maintain a growth mindset and help others learn
- **Quality**: Maintain high standards in educational content and code

### Community Standards

#### Expected Behavior

✅ **DO:**
- Welcome beginners and provide patient, helpful guidance
- Ask clarifying questions when concepts are unclear
- Share knowledge and resources generously
- Provide constructive feedback on contributions
- Respect different learning styles and paces
- Use inclusive language and be mindful of cultural differences
- Acknowledge and celebrate others' achievements

#### Unacceptable Behavior

❌ **DON'T:**
- Use offensive, discriminatory, or inappropriate language
- Dismiss or belittle questions, no matter how basic
- Engage in personal attacks or harassment
- Share solutions without explanation (robbing learning opportunities)
- Gatekeep or create artificial barriers for newcomers
- Spam or flood channels with unrelated content
- Violate academic integrity by submitting others' work as your own

### Learning Environment Guidelines

#### For Students

1. **Honor Your Learning Process**
   - Attempt problems independently before seeking help
   - Ask for guidance rather than complete solutions
   - Document your learning journey
   - Help others when you can

2. **Academic Integrity**
   - Understand concepts rather than just completing tasks
   - Cite sources and acknowledge help received
   - Create original work based on learning examples
   - Report academic dishonesty when observed

3. **Collaboration vs. Individual Work**
   - Discuss concepts and approaches (collaboration)
   - Write code independently after understanding (individual)
   - Share learning resources and insights (collaboration)
   - Submit identical solutions without understanding (plagiarism)

#### For Mentors and Tutors

1. **Effective Teaching**
   - Guide students to discover solutions rather than providing them
   - Use the Socratic method: ask probing questions
   - Break complex problems into smaller, manageable parts
   - Celebrate progress and effort, not just results

2. **Support and Feedback**
   - Provide specific, actionable feedback
   - Focus on concepts rather than just syntax
   - Offer multiple perspectives on problem-solving approaches
   - Maintain patience with different learning speeds

### Communication Guidelines

#### Forums and Discussions

- **Search First**: Check if your question has been answered before posting
- **Descriptive Titles**: Use clear, descriptive titles for questions and discussions
- **Structured Questions**: Include context, what you've tried, and specific problems
- **Code Formatting**: Use proper code blocks and syntax highlighting
- **Patience**: Allow time for responses; be patient with others' questions

#### Real-time Channels (Discord, Chat)

- **Be Present**: Stay engaged when participating in live discussions
- **Use Threads**: Keep conversations organized in threads when possible
- **Voice Etiquette**: Mute when not speaking in voice channels
- **Screen Sharing**: Ask permission before sharing your screen

#### Code Reviews

- **Be Kind**: Start reviews with positive observations
- **Be Specific**: Point out specific lines and explain why
- **Suggest Alternatives**: Offer better approaches rather than just criticism
- **Acknowledge Effort**: Recognize improvements and learning progress

### Academic Partnership Guidelines

#### For Universities

1. **Course Integration**
   - Adapt materials to fit your curriculum requirements
   - Attribute MultiOS educational materials appropriately
   - Share successful integration strategies with the community
   - Provide feedback on educational effectiveness

2. **Research Collaboration**
   - Collaborate on research projects using MultiOS as a platform
   - Share research findings back with the community
   - Mentor student contributors appropriately
   - Maintain ethical research practices

#### For Educators

1. **Material Adaptation**
   - Customize examples and exercises for your students
   - Provide additional scaffolding for your specific context
   - Report bugs or issues with educational materials
   - Contribute improvements back to the community

### Content Contribution Guidelines

#### Tutorial and Documentation Writers

1. **Quality Standards**
   - Test all code examples and instructions
   - Include clear learning objectives
   - Provide exercises and reflection questions
   - Use consistent formatting and style

2. **Accessibility**
   - Write for international audiences
   - Use inclusive examples and representations
   - Provide alternatives for different learning styles
   - Include accessibility considerations in examples

#### Code Contributors

1. **Educational Code Standards**
   - Prioritize clarity over cleverness
   - Include comprehensive comments explaining concepts
   - Follow idiomatic Rust practices
   - Provide multiple examples showing different approaches

### Reporting Issues

#### Code of Conduct Violations

If you witness or experience behavior that violates these guidelines:

1. **Immediate Response**: Contact a community moderator
2. **Documentation**: Document the incident (when, what, who)
3. **Privacy**: Respect the privacy of those involved
4. **Follow-up**: Cooperate with any investigation

#### Technical Issues

For bugs in educational materials:

1. **Reproduce**: Try to reproduce the issue
2. **Document**: Provide clear steps to reproduce
3. **Environment**: Include system and version information
4. **Expected vs Actual**: Clearly describe expected and actual behavior

### Enforcement

#### Warning System

1. **First Violation**: Written warning and private conversation
2. **Second Violation**: Temporary suspension (24 hours to 1 week)
3. **Third Violation**: Extended suspension or permanent ban
4. **Severe Violations**: Immediate permanent ban

#### Appeals Process

- Appeals can be submitted to the education committee
- Appeals will be reviewed within 5 business days
- Decisions are final but can be revisited if new evidence emerges
